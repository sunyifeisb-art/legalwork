# Skill: Identify Issues in Counterparty WARN Act Motion Brief

## 1. Subject-matter triage

- Treat the task as a defense-side issue-spotting exercise, not a merits brief; the output should isolate weaknesses in the plaintiff’s motion and map each weakness to an opposition strategy.
- First determine whether the motion turns on coverage, exception scope, notice adequacy, damages, declaration admissibility, or disputed facts; then analyze each category separately rather than blending them into one pass.
- If the record contains multiple layoffs, notice events, plants, or time periods, enumerate them before analyzing whether they must be aggregated or treated separately.

## 2. Failure modes the skill is correcting

- Analyst accepts the plaintiff’s legal theory without testing whether the asserted WARN framework fits the type of employment action and notice dispute actually in the record.
- Analyst accepts cited authority, quotations, or propositions without verifying accuracy, current validity, and fit to the issue presented.
- Analyst overlooks that a purportedly undisputed fact is unsupported, contradicted, or dependent on an inference the evidence does not establish.
- Analyst fails to challenge declarations that contain hearsay, legal conclusions, speculation, or statements outside personal knowledge.
- Analyst reviews damages or headcount calculations without independently checking the inputs, covered population, time period, and statutory method.
- Analyst stops at identifying defects and does not convert them into concrete opposition moves.

## 3. Legal frameworks / domain conventions that apply

- WARN Act coverage and exception analysis: determine the asserted statutory basis, identify the governing notice rule or exception, and test whether the motion applies it to the correct category of reduction in force.
- Notice sufficiency under shortened-notice theories: evaluate whether notice was given to the extent practicable and whether the motion accounts for any notice actually provided.
- Statutory damages framework: verify the correct WARN measure for back pay and benefits, the relevant violation period, and any statutory cap or limitation that governs the requested relief.
- Threshold and aggregation analysis: assess whether employment losses must be combined within the relevant window and whether prior workforce reductions or related events affect coverage.
- Summary-judgment evidence rules: declarations must be based on personal knowledge and admissible facts; legal conclusions, advocacy, and unsupported assumptions are objectionable under Rule 56 and the declaration rules.
- Citation-verification duty: every legal proposition relied upon should be tied to controlling authority by name and section, and every cited case should be checked for proposition accuracy and current status.
- Material-fact discipline: every “undisputed” fact should be matched to the cited record evidence and tested against any contrary evidence in the source set.

## 4. Analytical scaffolds

- Issue-by-issue legal deficiency analysis: state the plaintiff’s argument, identify the defect, give the controlling authority, and explain how the defect supports opposition.
- Authority verification pass: check each cited case or rule for the exact proposition used, and flag any quotation mismatch, limitation, or doctrinal drift.
- Evidentiary sufficiency pass: for each declaration or exhibit, determine whether it supports the stated fact, whether the fact is actually disputed, and whether an objection or motion to strike is warranted.
- Damages verification pass: independently test the plaintiff’s arithmetic against the underlying employee and compensation records, then identify the most defensible alternative calculation or methodological objection.
- Aggregation pass: examine whether prior reductions in force, overlapping notice periods, or related workforce events alter the threshold analysis or weaken the plaintiff’s framing.
- Opposition-strategy pass: translate each defect into a litigation move such as denial of summary judgment, narrowing of the issue, evidentiary objection, factual dispute, or alternative damages theory.

## 5. Vertical / structural / temporal relationships

- Compare the challenged event to any earlier or later layoffs, notices, or organizational changes in the record; the timing relationship may determine aggregation, coverage, or causation.
- Compare the plaintiff’s headcount, affected-employee set, and time period to the source documents; discrepancies in scope often create dispositive disputes.
- Compare each declaration statement to the supporting exhibit it cites; if the exhibit does not match the proposition, the fact should be treated as disputed.
- Compare the requested relief period to the alleged violation period; if the motion stretches beyond the supported timeframe, flag the mismatch.
- Where the motion relies on a sequence of events, test whether the chronology is complete and whether omitted events weaken the causation or notice theory.

## 6. Output structure conventions

- Produce an issue-identification memorandum organized by numbered issues with an ordinal severity field defined once at the top and applied uniformly to every issue entry.
- Use a severity scale such as Critical / High / Medium / Low, and assign the level based on litigation consequence and how close the defect comes to defeating the motion.
- For each issue, use the following internal sequence: adversary argument; deficiency identified; controlling legal authority; evidence or record point; recommended opposition strategy; severity.
- Make the most dispositive issues appear first.
- Each issue should do three things before closing: relate the point to a concrete scale in the record, cross-reference any interacting document or record item, and state the practical consequence for the defendant.
- Include objections to unsupported facts, improper conclusions, and unreliable declarations wherever they materially aid the opposition.
- End with a Recommended Actions block that converts the analysis into immediate drafting steps, using imperative verbs, a responsible role, and a timing anchor tied to the briefing schedule or hearing date.
- Include a short recommended opposition structure that groups the arguments in a logical order for a responsive brief.
