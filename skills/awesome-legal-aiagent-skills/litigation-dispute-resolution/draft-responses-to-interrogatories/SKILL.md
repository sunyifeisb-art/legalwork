# Skill: Draft Verified Responses and Objections to Plaintiff's First Set of Interrogatories in Commercial Breach of Contract and Fraud Action

## 2. Failure modes the skill is correcting

- Objecting in blanket terms instead of tying each objection to the interrogatory’s actual scope, while still answering any separable non-objectionable portion
- Treating contention-style interrogatories as optional or too early to answer, rather than responding based on current knowledge and noting supplementation where appropriate
- Using a business-records answer only when the cited records actually contain the responsive information and are described with enough specificity to be usable
- Failing to distinguish between attorney-signed objections and party-verified answers, or omitting the verification page entirely
- Writing answers that overstate certainty, guess at facts, or ignore the client interview memo and document set
- Leaving identification requests vague where names, dates, transactions, communications, or documents are available from the file
- Forgetting that incomplete or materially inaccurate answers must be corrected or supplemented when later information is obtained

## 3. Legal frameworks / domain conventions that apply

- Each interrogatory should be answered separately and fully in writing, with objections stated specifically and limited to the interrogatory’s proper scope
- Objections should preserve privilege, work-product, overbreadth, irrelevance, vagueness, ambiguity, proportionality, and undue burden only when the facts support them
- An objection does not excuse a response to the non-objectionable portion of the interrogatory unless the entire request is properly objectionable
- Interrogatory answers are ordinarily signed by the party under oath, while objections are signed by counsel
- Business-records responses are appropriate only if the records are identified with sufficient detail and the responding party can rely on them consistently with the applicable discovery rule governing interrogatory answers
- Contention interrogatories are generally answered from the current factual record; if discovery is incomplete, frame the answer as based on presently available information and preserve supplementation
- Supplementation remains necessary if later information makes a prior response incomplete or materially inaccurate
- Boilerplate objections without application to the particular interrogatory are weak and often disfavored

## 4. Analytical scaffolds

- Read the interrogatories one by one and classify each as asking for: factual narrative, identification of persons or documents, chronology, contention basis, admission/denial, calculation, or legal position
- For each interrogatory, decide whether the proper response is: full answer, qualified answer, objection plus partial answer, or objection with a business-records designation
- Use the client interview memo, pleadings, and collected documents to extract only supported facts; do not fill gaps with assumptions
- If the interrogatory asks for dates, participants, communications, transaction terms, or loss facts, compile the responsive universe before drafting the answer
- If multiple actors, events, agreements, or time periods are implicated, enumerate them internally before drafting so the response tracks each distinct item without collapsing them into a single generalized statement
- For contention interrogatories, state the factual basis presently known for the client’s position, identify the principal documents or categories of information supporting it, and reserve supplementation if the record develops
- Where the answer depends on records rather than memory, identify the records with enough specificity to locate them and confirm that they are as available to the propounding party as to the responding party if that rule is being invoked
- Draft each objection narrowly, then answer the remainder of the interrogatory to the extent it is not objectionable
- Keep the tone factual and disciplined; avoid advocacy, narrative sprawl, or unnecessary argument
- Ensure the final set of responses can be signed by the client representative and supported by the case file

## 5. Vertical / structural / temporal relationships

- Track each interrogatory against the underlying transaction chronology, so dates, communications, performance issues, and alleged misrepresentations are answered in the order they arose
- When an interrogatory spans multiple contracts, communications, actors, or decision points, break the response into subparts that follow the same sequence rather than compressing the facts into one paragraph
- Distinguish historical facts already known from positions still subject to discovery, especially for fraud allegations, contract performance disputes, damages, and affirmative-defense bases
- Where later events may alter an earlier response, note the present state of knowledge and preserve the duty to supplement rather than pretending the record is complete

## 6. Output structure conventions

- Use a standard discovery-response format: caption; preliminary/general objections only if needed; numbered responses matching the interrogatory numbers; verification page
- Keep each response aligned to its corresponding interrogatory number and preserve the numbering/order from the request
- When asserting an objection, state the specific ground first, then provide any responsive answer that remains available
- Use consistent discovery language such as “subject to,” “without waiving,” and “based on information presently available” only when genuinely needed and not as filler
- Do not include a separate narrative memo unless the task specifically requests one
- The completed document must be written as the operative response file named exactly `interrogatory-responses.docx`
