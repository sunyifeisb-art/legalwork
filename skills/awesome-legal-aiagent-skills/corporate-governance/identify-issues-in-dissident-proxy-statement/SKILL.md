# Skill: Defensive Advisory Memorandum — Identifying Issues in a Dissident Proxy Statement

## 1. Subject-matter triage
- Determine whether the materials concern a proxy contest, consent solicitation, nomination effort, or shareholder proposal path; use the governing procedural track before analyzing defects.
- Identify all persons, entities, filings, and solicitation documents in scope before analysis. If multiple dissident actors, filings, dates, or positions exist, enumerate them first and analyze each separately.
- Separate company-governance defects from SEC disclosure defects and from purely strategic counter-messaging points.

## 2. Failure modes the skill is correcting
- The memo lists defects but does not tie each one to a governing rule, a document conflict, and a practical response.
- The analysis overlooks derivative exposure, synthetic positions, or other non-traditional holdings that may affect beneficial-ownership disclosure.
- The memo accepts performance charts, comparison tables, or market claims without independently testing whether they are sourced, comparable, and not cherry-picked.
- The analysis misses voting-standard inconsistencies, which can distort solicitation framing and credibility.
- The memo flags a defect without showing why it matters now, what document it interacts with, or what the company can do next.
- The memo states a legal conclusion without naming the controlling authority supporting it.

## 3. Legal frameworks / domain conventions that apply
- **Beneficial ownership and solicitation disclosure:** Apply the relevant beneficial-ownership reporting regime, including rules governing direct and indirect ownership, shared voting or investment power, and derivative or synthetic exposure. Treat material changes and purpose changes as possible amendment triggers under the governing disclosure rule.
- **Group formation and coordinated action:** Evaluate whether parallel holdings, shared strategy, joint outreach, or coordinated solicitation conduct support a group-style disclosure analysis under the applicable securities rules and case law.
- **Proxy-solicitation anti-fraud principles:** Test the dissident’s materials against the anti-fraud standard for proxy solicitations under the federal securities rules. Flag statements that are materially false, misleading, incomplete, or unsupported by the underlying record.
- **Contest mechanics and voting standards:** Compare the dissident’s description of the voting standard, nomination mechanics, and election rules against the issuer’s governing documents and the applicable state-law framework.
- **Advance notice and nomination procedures:** If the materials are part of a nomination or proposal effort, test timing, content, ownership, disclosure, and form requirements against the governing bylaws and related notice provisions.
- **Shareholder proposal rules, if applicable:** Where the solicitation uses a shareholder-proposal pathway, assess compliance with eligibility, submission timing, length, supporting statement limits, resubmission rules, and exclusion-related requirements under the applicable SEC framework.
- **Controlling authority discipline:** For each conclusion, cite the governing rule, statute, regulation, bylaw provision, or leading authority that supports the point.

## 4. Analytical scaffolds
- **Disclosure compliance audit:** Review ownership filings, amendments, exhibit support, and solicitation materials together. Identify each position, instrument, and purpose statement; test whether the filing set captures the full economic exposure and control profile; then assess amendment timing against the material-change standard.
- **Coordination and group analysis:** Identify all evidence of coordination, shared messaging, parallel trading, common nominees, pooled expenses, or joint solicitation activity. Assess whether those facts create a disclosure issue, a credibility issue, or both.
- **Statement-verification pass:** For each factual, comparative, or performance claim, trace the assertion to a source document or public record. Verify that the comparison universe is relevant, the metrics are defined consistently, and the arithmetic is accurate.
- **Voting-standard verification:** Extract the governing voting mechanics and compare them to the dissident’s description. Flag any mismatch, omission, or simplification that could misstate the threshold or practical path to victory.
- **Procedural compliance check:** Compare the notice, nomination, or proposal package against the applicable procedural requirements. Identify missing information, late delivery, defective form, or inconsistent disclosures.
- **Issue-closing discipline:** For each issue, state the size, duration, or scope implicated; cross-reference the interacting filing, bylaw, or solicitation document; and explain the company consequence, such as disclosure remediation, regulatory outreach, litigation positioning, or counter-solicitation messaging.
- **Response mapping:** Assign each defect a concrete defensive response category, such as fact-checking, supplemental disclosure request, notice objection, proxy-advisor outreach, regulator outreach, or targeted counter-communication.

## 5. Vertical / structural / temporal relationships
- Build the timeline from the record date, notice deadline, filing dates, amendment dates, meeting date, and solicitation start date.
- Treat timing as part of the defect analysis: a technically curable defect may still be strategically important if the response window is short.
- Where multiple holders, filings, or instruments exist, analyze them in sequence and note how later disclosures may cure, confirm, or worsen earlier omissions.
- Consider how public disclosure timing may affect proxy-advisor review and the company’s defensive messaging window.

## 6. Output structure conventions
- Draft a defensive advisory memorandum in conventional issue-list form, organized by issue category and ordered by urgency or strategic significance.
- Define a simple ordinal severity scale at the top and apply it uniformly to every issue.
- For each issue, include: severity; short issue title; governing authority; the specific source evidence; why the issue is material; the cross-reference to the interacting document, filing, or bylaw provision; and the recommended company response.
- Close each issue with the practical consequence to the company, not just the defect description.
- End with a distinct Recommended Actions section that assigns an imperative action, the responsible role, and a timing anchor tied to the filing, notice, or meeting schedule.
