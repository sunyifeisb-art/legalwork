# Skill: Markup and Commentary Memo for a Proposed Regulatory Settlement Agreement

## 1. Subject-matter triage

- Treat the proposed settlement as both a drafting exercise and a compliance review: first identify the provisions that change legal exposure, then draft the markup, then write the commentary memo.
- If multiple monetary theories, conduct periods, admissions buckets, cooperation obligations, or oversight provisions are in play, enumerate them before analysis and handle each separately.
- Confirm the board-authorized parameters before editing substantive terms; any provision outside those bounds must be flagged as needing additional approval.
- Prioritize provisions that affect penalties, disgorgement, admissions, cooperation, monitorship, release scope, and timing.

## 2. Failure modes the skill is correcting

- The markup addresses headline terms but does not test whether the monetary-relief methodology complies with governing enforcement-law limits or whether older conduct is time-barred.
- The analysis fails to distinguish between supported and overbroad culpability characterizations in FCPA settlement language.
- Admissions are left broad enough to increase derivative-litigation and scienter risk without a targeted narrowing strategy.
- Cooperation language is treated as uniformly beneficial rather than as a potential source of prejudice in parallel proceedings.
- Monitor provisions are accepted without a clear dispute path, scope limit, or fee-review mechanism.
- The commentary memo states concerns without tying them to governing authority, source materials, or concrete revision language.
- Drafting relies only on visual redline styling instead of plain-text markup that survives export and review.

## 3. Legal frameworks / domain conventions that apply

- **Monetary relief methodology:** Test whether the proposed monetary relief is framed consistently with applicable limits on disgorgement, penalties, offsets, deductions, and causation under governing enforcement law.
- **Limitations-period analysis:** Identify the operative trigger for the look-back window, then exclude conduct or relief demands that fall outside the applicable limitations period.
- **FCPA penalty framework:** Assess whether the proposed tier or culpability characterization is supported by the alleged facts and the record supplied in the source materials.
- **Admissions and derivative exposure:** Narrow statements that imply management knowledge, board awareness, or compliance-system inadequacy unless the record and the settlement objective require that formulation.
- **Parallel-proceeding risk:** Cooperation clauses should be limited so the company is not compelled to prejudice itself in another governmental matter.
- **Monitor scope and fee disputes:** Define the monitor’s authority, preserve an objection path, and avoid giving the agency or monitor unilateral control over disputed work or fees.
- **Board authorization constraints:** Settlement language must fit within approved authority, financial ceilings, and substantive conditions in the board materials.
- For every legal proposition used in the markup or memo, cite the controlling authority by name and section when available in the source materials or generally recognized by practice.

## 4. Analytical scaffolds

- **Redline drafting method:** For each substantive edit, use a plain-text convention that remains readable outside Word styling: [DELETED: …], [INSERTED: …], or [REPLACED: old → new], followed immediately by a short [Rationale: …] note.
- **Monetary-relief review:** Identify the conduct period, determine the relevant limitations window, test the proposed figure against the governing relief theory, and note any cross-reference to schedules, exhibits, or factual recitations that drive the number.
- **Admissions audit:** Review each finding, recital, and acknowledgment for scienter, oversight, or derivative-litigation implications; draft narrower alternatives that preserve settlement viability while reducing collateral risk.
- **Cooperation-clause review:** Identify each compelled act, assess whether it reaches beyond the settlement matter, and add a carve-out or qualifier for parallel proceedings where needed.
- **Monitor review:** Check the scope of access, reporting, remediation demands, and fee treatment; add a dispute-resolution process and a reasonableness review for extraordinary demands.
- **Authority cross-check:** Tie each concern to the board-resolution parameters and any cited statute, regulation, rule, or case in the source set.
- **Commentary memo method:** Every issue entry should include the problem, governing basis, proposed revision, and the likely regulator response with the company’s answer.

## 5. Vertical / structural / temporal relationships

- Map the settlement terms against the board authorization to confirm which provisions are mandatory, negotiable, or outside delegated authority.
- Track how monetary relief, admissions, cooperation, and monitor provisions interact with each other; a concession in one section may amplify exposure in another.
- Separate conduct periods from filing periods and settlement performance periods; do not treat them as interchangeable.
- If the source set contains multiple alleged time periods or multiple distinct misconduct themes, analyze each on its own timeline rather than collapsing them into a single narrative.
- Where a clause references another agreement, schedule, or undertaking, check the cross-reference for hidden obligations or mismatched scope.

## 6. Output structure conventions

- Produce the redlined settlement agreement as the primary deliverable, then the commentary memo as the secondary deliverable.
- Ensure the redline is operatively complete in the document itself; the memo may not substitute for missing markup.
- In the redlined agreement, every substantive change must be identifiable from the text alone and accompanied by a short rationale comment.
- In the commentary memo, organize issues by priority using a clear ordinal severity scale defined once at the top, such as Critical / High / Medium / Low, and apply it consistently.
- Each memo entry should close with the requested revision and a concise anticipated-agency-response section.
- When more than one issue exists, list them separately and preserve the order of importance rather than blending distinct concerns.
- End the memo with a Recommended Actions block that uses imperative verbs, names the responsible role, and ties each action to a deadline or regulatory milestone in the source materials.
- Before finishing, verify that `redlined-settlement-agreement.docx` and `settlement-markup-commentary-memo.docx` each exist, are non-empty, and contain operative content rather than a summary of the assignment.
