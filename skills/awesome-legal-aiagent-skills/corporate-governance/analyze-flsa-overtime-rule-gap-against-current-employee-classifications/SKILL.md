# Skill: FLSA Overtime Rule Gap Analysis Against Employee Classifications

## 1. Subject-matter triage

- Treat the source set as a classification-and-remediation exercise, not a generic payroll summary.
- Start by separating employees by exemption category, jurisdiction, pay basis, and implementation status.
- If the materials span multiple threshold regimes or effective dates, analyze each regime and date band separately rather than blending them.
- If the record is incomplete on actual duties, preserve the issue as an open classification risk and identify what additional facts are needed.

## 2. Failure modes the skill is correcting

- Applying the salary level test without the duties test, or vice versa, and treating partial compliance as exempt status.
- Using a single federal threshold without checking whether a stricter state threshold or later-effective rule applies to a given employee.
- Assuming the job title controls when the audit memo or job description suggests the actual duties differ from the stated exemption basis.
- Ignoring prior audit findings that create notice of a classification issue and therefore affect retroactive exposure and good-faith arguments.
- Missing the effect of payroll conversion timing, transition periods, or delayed policy rollout on the gap window.
- Recommending salary increases without confirming that the compensation policy permits the change or that budget authority exists.
- Concluding that an employee is exempt based on compensation alone when the salary-basis requirement is not satisfied.

## 3. Legal frameworks / domain conventions that apply

- FLSA white-collar exemptions: executive, administrative, professional, and highly compensated employee exemptions each require satisfaction of the salary-basis/level test and the applicable duties test.
- Executive exemption: primary duty must be management of the enterprise or a recognized subdivision; the employee must customarily and regularly direct two or more employees; and the employee must have hiring/firing authority or meaningful input under 29 C.F.R. § 541.100.
- Administrative exemption: primary duty must be office or non-manual work related to management or general business operations, with discretion and independent judgment on matters of significance under 29 C.F.R. § 541.200.
- Professional exemption: primary duty must require advanced knowledge customarily acquired through prolonged specialized instruction, or invention, imagination, originality, or talent in a recognized artistic field under 29 C.F.R. § 541.300.
- Highly compensated employee exemption: elevated salary threshold plus customarily and regularly performing at least one exempt duty under 29 C.F.R. § 541.601.
- Salary basis requirement: fixed pay not subject to reduction based on quality or quantity of work under 29 C.F.R. § 541.602.
- Improper deductions and safe-harbor concepts should be assessed under the salary-basis regulations before concluding a classification defect.
- FLSA overtime rules and exemptions arise under 29 U.S.C. § 207 and 29 U.S.C. § 213.
- Regulatory threshold changes must be applied according to the rule in effect for the relevant period, including any phase-in or automatic update mechanism described in the briefing.
- State law may impose a higher salary threshold or narrower exemption than federal law; apply the more protective rule to employees in that jurisdiction.
- Retroactive exposure analysis should account for 29 U.S.C. § 255 limitations periods and whether the record supports a willfulness finding.
- Liquidated damages and good-faith mitigation should be evaluated under 29 U.S.C. § 216(b) and the FLSA good-faith defense framework.
- Any remediation plan should distinguish prospective reclassification from salary adjustment, duty restructuring, or other compliant pathways.

## 4. Analytical scaffolds

- Enumerate the workforce by exemption type, state, and pay status before analyzing any individual classification.
- For each exemption category, compare current compensation against the applicable threshold for the relevant time period and jurisdiction.
- For each position below or near threshold, test whether the job description and audit findings support every required element of the claimed exemption.
- For each potentially misclassified role, state the threshold comparison, the interacting source document that bears on duties or pay structure, and the downstream consequence for wages, operations, or legal exposure.
- Treat prior audit observations as a separate fact source: if they conflict with current classification labels, flag the inconsistency and explain why it matters.
- For positions with ambiguous duties, identify the missing facts that would resolve whether the exemption applies and recommend targeted follow-up.
- If the materials include average overtime patterns or estimated hours, use them to frame prospective exposure and remediation economics without inventing missing arithmetic.
- If a payroll conversion or policy implementation is pending, identify the period in which current classifications remain active and the gap this creates.
- If the highly compensated employee threshold is in play, assess whether a compensation increase is operationally preferable to reclassification only after confirming duties support that route.
- If the briefing includes an automatic threshold adjustment, note the budget-planning and reforecast implications for future review cycles.

## 5. Vertical / structural / temporal relationships

- Prior audit findings can supply constructive knowledge; unresolved findings may support a willfulness argument and a longer limitations period.
- The current job description is not dispositive if the audit memo describes materially different actual duties.
- Compensation policy approval constraints can limit whether a salary-based remediation is feasible on the desired timeline.
- State thresholds apply employee by employee, not enterprise-wide; one compliant location does not cure a higher-threshold jurisdiction.
- Transition periods, effective dates, and conversion milestones define the exposure window and should be traced chronologically.
- If the record suggests deduction practices inconsistent with salary basis, evaluate whether the issue affects only an individual or a broader classification cohort.

## 6. Output structure conventions

- Draft as an FLSA gap analysis memorandum with a short executive summary, followed by grouped analysis by exemption category or employee group.
- Include a simple ordinal severity scale at the outset and apply it consistently to each issue or position group.
- For each issue or group, state: current classification, applicable threshold, threshold comparison, duties-test assessment, source-document cross-reference, and consequence.
- Distinguish confirmed misclassification, likely misclassification, and items requiring further factual development.
- Include a separate section for retroactive exposure covering limitations period, willfulness indicators, and liquidated damages considerations.
- Include a separate section for remediation recommendations that prioritizes immediate fixes, near-term review items, and longer-horizon policy or budget actions.
- Every recommendation should identify the responsible internal role and tie timing to a concrete milestone or urgency anchor from the materials.
- Conclude with a concise implementation roadmap that coordinates payroll, compensation, HR, and legal review.
