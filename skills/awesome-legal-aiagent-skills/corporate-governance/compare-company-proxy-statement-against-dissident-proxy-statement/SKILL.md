# Skill: Company vs. Dissident Proxy Statement Comparative Analysis

## 1. Subject-matter triage (only if applicable)

- Identify the contest posture first: contested election, solicitation over a proposal, or a mixed proxy fight with governance and economic themes.
- Separate the source set into company materials, dissident materials, and the client engagement memo; treat the engagement memo as the frame for relevance, not as proof of contested facts.
- Enumerate the key comparison axes before analysis: voting mechanics, financial claims, ownership claims, nominee profiles, governance history, and disclosed risks.
- If only one nominee class, one voting issue, or one metric is actually in dispute, state that affirmatively and confine the analysis to that scope.

## 2. Failure modes the skill is correcting

- Accepting proxy assertions at face value without cross-checking them against public filings, prior statements, or internal consistency across the source set.
- Treating definitional differences as if they were errors, or missing real discrepancies because the same concept is labeled differently across the company and dissident materials.
- Overlooking material omissions in dissident materials, especially where the omitted fact could alter how a reasonable shareholder evaluates the campaign.
- Failing to connect historical shareholder support, governance dissatisfaction, and current solicitation themes into one contest-dynamics assessment.
- Misstating the applicable voting standard, record date mechanics, or election structure, which can distort the practical implications of the solicitation.
- Comparing nominees only narratively rather than in a disciplined side-by-side format tied to the business and governance issues in dispute.

## 3. Legal frameworks / domain conventions that apply

- Proxy solicitations are assessed under the federal anti-fraud standard for proxy materials, including whether statements are materially false or misleading or omit a material fact; cite Exchange Act Section 14(a) and SEC Rule 14a-9.
- Materiality turns on whether a reasonable shareholder would view the fact as significantly altering the total mix of information; cite the controlling materiality standard recognized in securities law.
- Director-election mechanics depend on the governing charter, bylaws, and proxy disclosures; confirm whether the election is plurality, majority, or another stated standard, and verify that the materials describe it consistently.
- Disclosure about nominees should be compared for completeness, relevance, and conflicts using ordinary proxy practice conventions, not just biography length.
- Shareholder voting history on compensation or other governance items can be a meaningful signal of investor sentiment and should be read in the context of the current contest narrative.
- Financial metrics in proxy materials may use adjusted definitions, alternative denominators, or non-GAAP style presentations; verify the underlying basis before labeling a statement wrong.
- Ownership claims should be checked against the denominator disclosed in the public record and against the actual share count basis used in the solicitation materials.

## 4. Analytical scaffolds

- Use a side-by-side comparison for each disputed topic: company statement, dissident statement, source-check result, and contest significance.
- For each financial claim, identify the metric, the source basis, the company’s framing, the dissident’s framing, and whether the difference is definitional, contextual, or potentially misleading.
- For each claimed omission, ask whether the omitted fact is adverse, disclosed elsewhere in the source set, and likely to matter to a reasonable shareholder under the proxy anti-fraud standard.
- For each ownership claim, reconcile the reported percentage with the disclosed share count basis and note any denominator issue, rounding issue, or apparent inconsistency.
- For each governance-history point, identify the prior vote, the level of support or opposition, and whether it supports a narrative of management dissatisfaction.
- For each nominee, compare experience, industry relevance, board service, independence, conflicts, and any campaign-related concerns; keep the comparison parallel and concise.
- For each voting-mechanics point, confirm the record date, meeting date, solicitation timing, voting standard, and practical effect on the outcome.
- Close each issue by tying together the scale of the point, the related source materials, and the likely consequence for the client’s voting or advocacy strategy.

## 5. Vertical / structural / temporal relationships (only if applicable)

- Treat the timeline as a central organizing device: record date, filing dates, solicitation windows, meeting date, and any response deadlines should be read together.
- Read historical voting support in light of the dissident’s current theory of the case; prior low support may strengthen a governance critique, while prior strong support may weaken it.
- Where both sides rely on the same financial metric, the more important question is not just correctness but whether the divergence changes the persuasive force of the contest narrative.
- If a disclosure appears in one filing but not the other, assess whether the omission is neutral background, strategic emphasis, or a potentially material gap.

## 6. Output structure conventions

- Write a governance analysis memorandum organized by conventional topic headings, such as contest overview, voting mechanics, financial verification, material omissions, ownership and support, nominee comparison, and contest dynamics.
- Define an ordinal severity scale once near the beginning of the memorandum and apply it uniformly to each issue or discrepancy.
- Use numbered sections or tables for each issue category so the reader can track one comparison path at a time.
- Include a side-by-side financial verification table with the company figure, dissident figure, source-check result, and practical significance.
- Include a side-by-side nominee comparison table that emphasizes qualifications, governance concerns, and campaign relevance.
- End with an explicit Recommended Actions section that assigns each step to a role drawn from the source materials and ties it to the relevant deadline or solicitation milestone.
- Cite the controlling legal authority for each legal proposition by name and section or rule, rather than stating conclusions in bare form.
- Keep the memorandum analytical rather than narrative: every identified discrepancy should state what it is, why it matters, and how it affects the contest.
