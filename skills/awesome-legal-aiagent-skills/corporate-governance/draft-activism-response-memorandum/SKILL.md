# Skill: Activism Response Memorandum for Board of Directors

## 2. Failure modes the skill is correcting

- Analyzing defensive options without first testing the legal, timing, proxy-advisory, and litigation constraints that determine whether the board can actually use them
- Treating the activist campaign as a single actor problem when filing status, coordination, voting power, and parallel objectives may differ across holders
- Recommending a separation, sale, or standalone path without adjusting for the economics of shared services, transfer arrangements, and replacement costs
- Missing executive employment consequences of board or strategy changes, especially where the activist seeks leadership changes, reporting-line changes, or compensation revisions
- Ignoring pending deal or financing overlays that can constrain board action, create covenant pressure, or alter the response strategy
- Offering conclusions without tracing them back to the controlling legal authority or the source-document facts that support them

## 3. Legal frameworks / domain conventions that apply

- Beneficial ownership disclosure regime: identify when an investor crosses the applicable ownership-disclosure threshold for a registered equity security, determine whether the required filing was timely, and assess procedural consequences of any delay
- Group-formation analysis: evaluate whether two or more persons have coordinated acquisition, voting, holding, or disposition activity in a way that could create a disclosure group, including informal coordination, parallel conduct, or shared objectives
- Classified board structure: if the corporation has a staggered board, confirm which director class is up for election, the number of seats available in the current cycle, and any charter or bylaw mechanics affecting declassification or expansion of board representation
- Heightened scrutiny for vote-interfering board action: if defensive board action is directed primarily at affecting the shareholder vote, assess the applicable heightened standard and the evidentiary showing needed to support the action
- Rights plan analysis: evaluate whether a rights plan is defensible in light of timing, purpose, and proxy-advisory reaction, including whether the plan may be viewed as preclusive or coercive and whether shareholder approval would improve the analysis
- Executive good-reason provisions: review senior executive agreements for provisions triggered by changes in duties, authority, compensation, reporting lines, or similar employment terms, and estimate any resulting severance or exit obligations
- Intercompany transfer-pricing and shared-services economics: identify intercompany arrangements supporting the target business, estimate the market-rate cost of replacing those services externally, and adjust valuation and cash-flow analysis accordingly
- Pending-transaction interaction analysis: determine whether activist board representation could affect the timing, approval path, or closing conditions of any pending transaction, including any regulatory or contractual constraints
- Credit agreement covenant review: assess leverage, financial-maintenance, and change-of-control provisions in applicable financing documents, and determine how any proposed board or capital-structure action may interact with them
- Delaware fiduciary-duty and takeover-defense principles: where the board adopts a defensive or vote-affecting measure, analyze under the governing state-law standard for proportionality, purpose, and evidentiary support
- Proxy-access, nomination, and annual-meeting mechanics: confirm notice windows, advance-notice requirements, record-date mechanics, and any charter or bylaw limitations affecting the activist’s path to board representation
- Proxy-advisory and institutional-voter conventions: assess likely voting policies, independence concerns, performance narratives, and capital-allocation themes that may shape major-holder support

## 4. Analytical scaffolds

- Shareholder landscape analysis: identify the major holders, their likely governance preferences, and their prior voting posture on board composition, capital allocation, separation proposals, and defense measures
- Disclosure-compliance analysis: identify the relevant acquisition and filing dates, compare them with the source-document timeline, and assess the procedural implications of any delay or coordination issue
- Group-formation analysis: separate evidence of actual agreement from mere similarity of views, and identify what facts in the record support or weaken a disclosure-group theory
- Board-structure analysis: determine the current election cycle, the seats realistically in play, and the structural limits on any attempted board refresh
- Defensive-options analysis: evaluate each proposed response against timing, purpose, likely proxy-advisory reaction, and the board’s evidentiary record; do not recommend a defense without explaining the constraint that makes it viable
- Fiduciary-duty analysis: if the board contemplates a measure affecting voting power or the election process, identify the governing state-law doctrine and the record needed to justify the action
- CEO employment-agreement analysis: identify the good-reason definition and applicable triggers, then assess whether any proposed activist demand or board action would implicate severance or retention risk
- Transfer-cost adjustment: identify the services, people, systems, and support functions that would have to be replaced, estimate market replacement cost, and reassess any standalone or separation narrative accordingly
- Pending-transaction analysis: assess whether activist nominees or strategy changes could affect the deal timetable, approvals, closing conditions, or regulatory path
- Credit-covenant analysis: assess covenant headroom, any change-of-control definition, and any practical financing consequence that could result from the activist’s proposal set
- Recommendation synthesis: translate each legal and economic constraint into a concrete board response, distinguishing immediate defensive steps from longer-term engagement or disclosure strategy

## 5. Vertical / structural / temporal relationships

- Timing relative to record dates, nomination windows, and meeting deadlines is legally significant; actions taken after those milestones may be harder to defend if they change the electoral process
- The shareholder landscape is the foundation for the response strategy; the board should understand likely alignment among major institutions, including governance policies, activism history, and views on capital deployment
- If multiple holders may be acting together, analyze the campaign across holders and time periods rather than as a single static position
- Where a pending transaction or financing exists, treat the activist response as part of a wider timeline that can affect closing risk, covenant pressure, and negotiation leverage

## 6. Output structure conventions

- Draft a privileged board advisory memorandum in conventional business-legal form, with an executive summary, shareholder landscape, activist vulnerabilities, defensive options and legal constraints, transaction and financing implications, management and succession issues, and recommended board actions
- Include a clear recommendation section that states what the board should do next, who should do it, and when it should occur relative to the next governance milestone
- For every substantive legal proposition, cite the controlling authority by name and section, or the governing doctrine if the authority is commonly recognized and no source-document citation is provided
- For every issue discussed, tie the legal point to the source-document facts, the relevant cross-document interaction, and the practical consequence for the board
- If the campaign involves more than one holder, proposal, date, or defensive measure, address each separately before synthesizing the response
- Keep the memo privilege-forward, decision-oriented, and operational; avoid generic activism commentary that is not tied to the record
