# Skill: Draft Formal Trustee Annual Accounting

## 1. Subject-matter triage (only if applicable)

- Treat the assignment as a formal fiduciary accounting, not a narrative summary.
- Determine at the outset whether the trust is governed by a specific trust instrument, a state principal-and-income statute, or both; the instrument controls where it departs from default rules.
- Identify the accounting period, opening balances, closing balances, and whether any interim events require separate treatment within the year.
- If the source set includes multiple statements, ledgers, tax forms, invoices, or closing packages, treat each as a distinct authority and reconcile them before drafting.

## 2. Failure modes the skill is correcting

- Including pass-through distributions as income without reviewing the underlying tax reporting to identify return-of-capital portions; return of capital reduces cost basis and should be treated as a principal adjustment rather than current income.
- Misallocating legal and advisory fees between income and principal without applying the governing principal-and-income framework; the nature of the service determines the proper allocation.
- Using an incorrect valuation for in-kind distributions to beneficiaries; value in-kind items as of the distribution date using supported market data.
- Omitting proration credits or similar adjustments that appear on closing statements for real property sales.
- Failing to separately identify tax-exempt income; it should appear as a distinct line item for beneficiary reporting and tax preparation.
- Accepting a prior accounting's figures without independently reconciling each line to source documents such as brokerage statements, tax forms, invoices, closing statements, and transaction ledgers.
- Collapsing multiple transactions, periods, or counterparties into one generalized review instead of testing each item separately.
- Drafting an issues memo that describes discrepancies without stating the source basis, the accounting figure, the corrected figure, and the client consequence.
- Omitting a clear severity designation in a discrepancy memo, which makes prioritization and remediation unclear.

## 3. Legal frameworks / domain conventions that apply

- Uniform Principal and Income Act or state equivalent: allocates receipts and disbursements between principal and income; dividends, ordinary interest, and rental income are typically income; capital gains, return of capital, and proceeds from asset sales are typically principal.
- Return of capital from pass-through entities: reported on the relevant tax reporting; reduces adjusted cost basis; should be reclassified from income to a principal basis adjustment.
- Legal fee allocation: fees for administration of the trust estate are generally charged to principal; fees for income-related services are generally charged to income; some jurisdictions specify an allocation rule — apply the governing jurisdiction's statute and reflect the authority in the notes.
- Investment advisory fees: many principal and income regimes allocate advisory fees between principal and income; absent a trust provision specifying otherwise, apply the statutory default.
- In-kind distributions: value at fair market value on the distribution date; the per-share or per-unit price used must be supported by market data as of that date.
- Accrued interest on fixed-income purchases: when a bond or similar instrument is purchased between coupon dates, the buyer pays the seller accrued interest; classify this as a principal item at purchase rather than an income disbursement.
- Tax-exempt income identification: tax-exempt interest must appear as a separate line item in the income schedule so it can be identified correctly for reporting purposes.
- Fiduciary accounting conventions: opening balances must tie to the prior closing balances, realized gains and losses must reconcile to sale activity, and distribution totals must reconcile to beneficiary activity and valuation support.
- Governing authority should be named whenever a legal allocation or valuation rule is applied; do not state the conclusion without the rule that supports it.

## 4. Analytical scaffolds

1. Reconcile every line item in the draft accounting against source documents before accepting any figure; brokerage statements, tax forms, invoices, closing statements, and the transaction ledger are the authoritative sources.
2. Enumerate each transaction category and each period-sensitive item separately before analysis: receipts, disbursements, gains and losses, distributions, fees, tax-exempt items, return-of-capital items, and any in-kind transfers or real-estate closing adjustments.
3. Review each tax form for pass-through entities held in the trust; identify return-of-capital components; reclassify to principal and adjust the asset's cost basis in the asset inventory schedule.
4. Review each legal invoice and classify the service as administration or income-related; reallocate fees that were charged to the wrong pool.
5. Verify all in-kind distributions in the distribution schedule against market data for the specific distribution date; recalculate the distribution value if the per-share or per-unit price used is incorrect.
6. Review real property closing statements for credits and proration amounts; confirm they are reflected in the correct schedules.
7. Verify that tax-exempt interest income is listed separately from taxable income in the income schedule.
8. Check that opening values, closing values, realized gains, and distributed values cross-foot across schedules without hidden balancing entries.
9. Compile an issues memorandum listing each discrepancy with the source document basis for the correction, the accounting figure, the corrected figure, the legal or accounting rule applied, the downstream effect, and a severity designation.
10. If only one item exists in a category, state that it is the sole item in scope before analyzing it; do not imply a broader review.

## 5. Vertical / structural / temporal relationships

- The trust instrument governs distribution standards, trustee fee provisions, and any deviations from statutory principal-income allocation defaults; review it before finalizing any allocation.
- The prior-year accounting establishes the beginning figures for the current accounting period; the opening values must match the prior year's closing values exactly.
- Tax forms and brokerage year-end statements are the authoritative sources for investment income, gains, and cost basis; internal ledger entries that conflict with these statements must be resolved.
- Sales, purchases, receipts, and distributions often have linked principal and income consequences; trace each event through the schedule set rather than treating schedules as independent.
- Temporal sequencing matters: valuation is measured as of the transaction or distribution date, while income classification follows the accrual or payment rule applicable to the instrument or expense.

## 6. Output structure conventions

- Produce two deliverables: the formal annual accounting first, then a separate issues memorandum.
- Draft the accounting as a court-ready fiduciary account with conventional schedules, not as a spreadsheet dump or a prose memo.
- Use standard accounting sections such as principal receipts and disbursements, income receipts and charges, asset inventory, realized gains and losses, distributions, and notes explaining methodology and allocation decisions.
- Include separate identification of tax-exempt income within the income presentation.
- Include clear beginning and ending balances for each relevant schedule and make the schedules internally consistent.
- In the issues memorandum, define a severity scale once near the top and use it consistently for every discrepancy.
- For each issue, include the accounting entry as presented, the source-supported figure, the discrepancy type, the governing rule or authority, the downstream consequence, and a concise recommendation for correction.
- End the issues memorandum with a Recommended Actions section that assigns the next step to the appropriate responsible role and ties it to a filing, review, or correction milestone.
- Ensure the primary accounting file is complete and non-empty before treating the memorandum as finished.
