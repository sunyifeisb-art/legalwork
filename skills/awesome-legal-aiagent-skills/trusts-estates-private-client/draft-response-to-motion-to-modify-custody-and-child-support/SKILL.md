# Skill: Draft Opposition Brief to Motion to Modify Custody and Child Support

## 1. Subject-matter triage

- Treat the requested relief as potentially multi-part: parenting time, legal decision-making or custody, and child support may each turn on different standards and records.
- Identify whether the motion seeks a true modification, a temporary adjustment, or a relabeling of existing orders; do not assume the requested change matches the caption.
- Separate threshold reopening issues from merits issues at the outset, because a failure on the threshold may dispose of some or all of the requested relief.

## 2. Failure modes the skill is correcting

- Failing to test whether the moving party has met the threshold for modification before addressing the requested change on the merits
- Accepting asserted income, expense, or earning-capacity figures without checking them against financial records, tax materials, and other source documents
- Responding to allegations in the abstract instead of rebutting them with record evidence tied to the specific claim
- Omitting a factor-by-factor best-interests analysis for each custody or parenting-time issue the motion raises
- Ignoring timing, continuity, and baseline-order issues that determine whether the asserted change is substantial, continuing, or stale
- Treating child support as a purely mechanical calculation without first verifying the inputs and the legal basis for recalculation
- Failing to distinguish temporary orders from final orders when describing what has changed and what remains in place
- Making legal conclusions without naming the governing rule, statute, or controlling case that supports them

## 3. Legal frameworks / domain conventions that apply

- Modification standard: start with the jurisdiction’s rule governing modification of custody, parenting time, and support, including any required showing of changed circumstances, material change, substantial and continuing change, or best-interests trigger
- Best interests: apply the governing statutory or common-law best-interests factors relevant to legal decision-making and parenting time, using the factors recognized in the jurisdiction
- Child support framework: apply the jurisdiction’s child support guidelines or equivalent statutory framework, including the rules for income determination, deviations, and recalculation
- Income attribution: where a parent is self-employed, underemployed, intermittently employed, or paid through a business, compare sworn statements to bank activity, tax filings, business records, and lifestyle evidence as appropriate
- Financial disclosure: reconcile pay statements, tax returns, profit-and-loss materials, account statements, and expense records before accepting any asserted support figure
- Evidentiary support: prefer contemporaneous records, official records, and authenticated exhibits over conclusory declarations when the motion turns on disputed facts
- Children’s preference: if the motion relies on an asserted preference, evaluate the evidentiary basis, age and maturity considerations, and any need for neutral fact-finding under the governing rules
- Allegations of interference or alienation: answer with concrete co-parenting records, communication logs, exchange history, school or medical coordination records, and compliance history
- Procedural prerequisites: check for missing declarations, required attachments, mandatory disclosures, timing requirements, or other local prerequisites that may defeat or narrow the motion
- Governing authority: cite the controlling statute, rule, regulation, or leading case for each legal proposition rather than relying on general statements of law

## 4. Analytical scaffolds

1. Identify each discrete request in the motion and track the movant’s order of presentation so the opposition answers the same issues in a clear sequence.
2. State the governing threshold for modification, then compare the asserted changed circumstances to the conditions at the time of the prior order.
3. Test whether the alleged change is supported by the record, whether it is substantial and continuing if required, and whether it is temporally connected to the filing date.
4. For each factual allegation, locate the most responsive exhibit or document in the source set and cite it specifically in the brief.
5. Build a parenting record affirmatively: schooling, medical care, therapy, exchanges, communication, attendance, transportation, and compliance with prior orders.
6. For each best-interests factor that applies, state the factor, apply the facts, and explain why the existing arrangement remains appropriate or why the requested change is not supported.
7. For support, determine the best-supported income inputs from the record, then test the requested modification against the guideline framework and any deviation rules.
8. If income attribution is disputed, compare sworn disclosures to banking, tax, business, or lifestyle evidence and explain any inconsistency in plain terms.
9. If the motion includes multiple parents, periods, children, or requested orders, analyze each one separately rather than collapsing them into a single blended response.
10. If the motion depends on a timeline, reconstruct the chronology from the baseline order through the alleged change and into the filing date; use that sequence to test continuity, staleness, and causation.

## 5. Vertical / structural / temporal relationships

- The prior decree or operative order is the baseline for every comparison; describe the existing arrangement precisely before discussing the requested change.
- Any temporary order entered after the baseline must be distinguished from the final order so the court can see what is truly being modified.
- The time between the alleged change and the motion filing matters; use that timeline to assess whether the claimed circumstance is ongoing, isolated, or already resolved.
- If the record contains multiple versions of schedules, parenting plans, or financial disclosures, identify which version governs and which version is merely historical.
- Where support and custody issues interact, note how a custody change would affect support only after the custody request is legally and factually established.

## 6. Output structure conventions

- Single deliverable: opposition brief for filing.
- Use a conventional brief structure: caption, preliminary statement or introduction, statement of governing standard, argument organized by issue, requested relief, and certificate of service.
- Organize the argument so each heading mirrors a request in the motion and each subpoint addresses a distinct factual or legal objection.
- Each legal proposition should be supported by a named authority, and each factual rebuttal should cite the relevant exhibit, declaration, record extract, or document by name.
- When discussing income or support, show the record-based inputs that support the opposition’s position and explain any inconsistency between the movant’s figures and the source materials.
- Conclude by requesting denial of the motion in whole or in part, or any narrower relief supported by the record.
