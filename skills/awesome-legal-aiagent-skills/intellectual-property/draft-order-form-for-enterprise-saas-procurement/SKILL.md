# Skill: Draft Order Form for Enterprise SaaS Procurement

## 1. Subject-matter triage
- Treat the order form as the operative commercial instrument under an existing master agreement, not as a standalone contract.
- Identify whether the procurement involves regulated, sensitive, or operationally critical data, because that may drive required order-level terms on privacy, security, audit, retention, use restrictions, and incident handling.
- Separate final terms from drafting inputs: pricing approvals, procurement emails, policy materials, data terms, and vendor paper may each supply different assumptions that must be reconciled.

## 2. Failure modes the skill is correcting
- Drafting the order form as if the master agreement alone governs, without reconciling pricing, data, policy, or approval documents that may alter order-level terms.
- Silently resolving inconsistencies instead of cataloging them in the issues memorandum for client decision-making.
- Missing order-form provisions needed for enterprise software procurement, including subscription scope, deployment model, support/SLA mechanics, data-use restrictions, security commitments, and any regulated-data handling.
- Allowing business terms, legal terms, and implementation assumptions to drift across documents without a clear hierarchy or override rule.
- Omitting an explicit recommendation for how each material inconsistency should be handled in the final papering package.

## 3. Legal frameworks / domain conventions that apply
- An order form under an existing master services agreement should identify the governing agreement, incorporate it by reference, and specify only the transaction-specific economics and scope unless the master agreement permits broader modifications.
- Enterprise SaaS order forms commonly set out subscription term, quantities, modules, permitted use, implementation assumptions, support level, fees, invoicing cadence, renewal mechanics, and any order-specific overrides.
- If privacy, security, data processing, or cross-border processing terms are implicated, the order form should align with the operative data processing documentation and the parties’ compliance posture under applicable privacy law and contract principles.
- For regulated or sensitive-use deployments, the drafting should check whether the platform’s intended use requires express commitments on access controls, audit rights, incident notification, retention/deletion, subcontractor flow-downs, and assistance obligations.
- Procurement policy and approval records may constrain vendor onboarding, contracting authority, price approval, or risk acceptance; those constraints should be reflected or flagged as unresolved.
- Any legal proposition used in the memo should be anchored to the controlling authority invoked by the source materials or the standard commercial-contract rule being applied; avoid unsupported conclusory statements.

## 4. Analytical scaffolds
- Start with a document hierarchy review: identify the master agreement, order form template, pricing sheet, data terms, procurement policy, approval trail, and any ancillary attachments.
- Build a clause-by-clause comparison of economics, scope, compliance, and operational commitments, then reconcile them against the master agreement’s amendment and precedence mechanics.
- For the order form, draft only the operative commercial and order-specific terms that are needed to make the transaction executable and internally consistent.
- For the issues memorandum, organize by topic area and for each item state: the conflicting sources, the inconsistency or gap, the effect on the transaction, and a proposed resolution.
- When multiple products, affiliates, sites, service tiers, regions, or effective dates appear in the source set, enumerate them explicitly before analysis and handle each one separately rather than collapsing them into a single generic pass.
- If a source set presents only one applicable item in a category, say so affirmatively and explain why no broader comparison is needed.
- For every issue flagged, include a severity label using a consistent ordinal scale defined once at the top of the memorandum.
- Close each issue with the practical consequence for the client and a concrete next step tied to the transaction timeline.
- End the advisory memorandum with a concise Recommended Actions section that assigns each action to a role and ties it to a milestone or urgency anchor.

## 5. Vertical / structural / temporal relationships
- Respect the hierarchy among the master agreement, order form, incorporated attachments, and any later-in-time approvals or amendments; state clearly which document controls where a conflict exists.
- Distinguish pre-signing approvals from post-signing operational obligations, and do not assume an approval email overrides executed contract text unless the papering supports that result.
- Track timing-sensitive items separately: order date, service start date, invoicing start, renewal date, notice windows, and any data-transfer or implementation milestones.
- If the procurement spans business, legal, security, and finance inputs, map the dependencies so the order form does not commit the client before the upstream approvals or documentation are aligned.

## 6. Output structure conventions
- Produce the order form as a complete execution-ready document under the existing master agreement, with operative clauses and defined terms only where needed for the transaction.
- Produce the issues memorandum as a separate advisory document with a defined severity scale, topic-based issue entries, and a final Recommended Actions section.
- Use conventional commercial-document headings rather than mirroring any internal checklist or rubric language.
- Keep the order form concise and the memorandum practical; do not embed process commentary where operative terms or client-facing recommendations belong.
- Before finishing, verify that the order form file exists and contains substantive operative terms, and that the issues memorandum exists and contains actual issue analysis and recommendations.
