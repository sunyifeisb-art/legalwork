# Skill: Draft Officer's Certificate for Auto Loan ABS Closing

## 1. Subject-matter triage

- Confirm whether the closing package calls for one certificate, separate certificates for different transaction parties, or a certificate with distinct sections for conditions precedent, pool compliance, and bring-down matters.
- Identify each governing document that imposes closing certifications, especially where the same eligibility concept appears in more than one document with different wording or thresholds.
- Determine whether the pool is static or replenishing, whether any special loan populations are included, and whether any collateral types trigger nonstandard perfection language.
- If the transaction includes multiple notes, classes, or series, treat each as a separate closing datum to be checked against the source materials before drafting.

## 2. Failure modes the skill is correcting

- Drafting a generic closing certificate that addresses only conditions precedent and omits pool-level eligibility representations.
- Collapsing distinct document-based thresholds into a single certification instead of tracking each governing source separately.
- Using generic secured-transactions language for motor vehicle collateral instead of the title-notation perfection concept that applies to receivables tied to vehicles.
- Failing to carry through issue-specific certifications that are called for by the transaction structure, closing instructions, or pool characteristics.
- Preparing an issues memo that identifies concerns but never translates them into an executable certificate draft.
- Writing a memo or certificate that discusses legal conclusions without tying them to the operative transaction-document authority.

## 3. Legal frameworks / domain conventions that apply

- Auto loan ABS officer’s certificates commonly perform two functions at closing: certify satisfaction of conditions precedent under the operative deal documents and certify that the receivables satisfy the applicable eligibility representations as of the cut-off date.
- Where more than one governing document addresses the same eligibility concept, the draft should preserve each document’s language and threshold rather than harmonizing them by implication.
- Motor vehicle receivables are typically perfected through lien notation on the certificate of title or equivalent title-system filing, not by generic UCC filing language alone.
- Per-obligor and per-receivable limitations are separate constraints and should be treated as distinct checks even when both concern exposure concentration.
- If the pool includes loans that experienced forbearance, the draft should account for any lookback, cure, or post-forbearance performance requirements reflected in the source documents.
- The signer’s authority should match the issuer’s organizational form and the certificate should use the appropriate corporate capacity language.
- Every operative legal proposition should be anchored to the controlling transaction document or other cited authority in the source set; do not state a certification without identifying the clause or rule it tracks.

## 4. Analytical scaffolds

1. Start with the deal heading: identify the issuer, transaction name, closing date, signatory, and signatory title in a way that matches the issuer’s corporate form.
2. Separate closing certifications into distinct buckets: conditions precedent, note issuance conditions, pool eligibility, collateral perfection, and any special bring-down or confirmation items.
3. Map each certification to its source authority before drafting: identify the document, section, schedule, or closing instruction that requires it.
4. For each eligibility concept, check whether the PSA, indenture, or other governing document uses different terminology, scope, or thresholds; preserve those differences in the draft.
5. Where the source materials present multiple notes, classes, or balances, draft each principal amount and related certification with its own label and no implied aggregation unless the documents require it.
6. If the pool includes special categories of receivables, test each category against the relevant representations, lookback periods, and cure conditions before inclusion.
7. Draft the perfection representation to match the collateral type and the actual perfection method reflected in the deal documents and title process.
8. Flag any pool item or closing condition that is near a threshold, ambiguous, or dependent on a final confirmation, and carry that issue into the memo.
9. Use the memo to explain drafting choices, identify open items, and record any items that should be confirmed before execution.
10. Ensure the certificate reads as a formal attestation, while the memo reads as a concise legal drafting note.

## 5. Vertical / structural / temporal relationships

- Treat the certificate as a closing-time snapshot: the relevant date is the cut-off or closing date identified in the source documents, and each representation should be tied to that date where applicable.
- Distinguish between pre-closing conditions, as-of-closing certifications, and post-closing deliverables so the certificate does not blur timing obligations.
- If the transaction documents distinguish the issuer, seller, servicer, or sponsor, assign each certification to the correct entity and do not assume one role satisfies another.
- Where a requirement depends on the state of title, registration, insurance, or third-party confirmation, identify whether the condition must be satisfied before execution or merely disclosed as outstanding.
- If multiple document layers govern the same fact, draft from the most specific source first and use the others to confirm consistency rather than overwrite them.
- When the final pool data and closing instructions differ, treat the discrepancy as an issue to resolve, not as a drafting cue to average the figures.

## 6. Output structure conventions

- Produce two deliverables: a formal officer’s certificate and a separate drafting memorandum.
- The officer’s certificate should use conventional certification form: caption, introductory recital, numbered certifications, qualifications or exceptions if supported by the source documents, and signature block.
- The certificate should read as operative drafting, not as commentary; avoid narrative explanation inside the certified statements unless the document requires it.
- The drafting memorandum should be concise but substantive: identify the source of each drafting choice, the provisions that control it, and any open questions or threshold-sensitive items.
- If more than one note class, receivable group, or certification set is in scope, list each separately in the draft rather than folding them into a single generic paragraph.
- Include any document-specific conditions precedent, third-party confirmations, or perfection-related statements only where the source materials support them.
- Use terminology that matches the transaction documents and the ABS market conventions reflected in the source set.
- Before finalizing, confirm that the primary artifact is fully drafted and that the memo does not substitute for missing certificate text.
