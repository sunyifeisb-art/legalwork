# Skill: Identify Issues in Draft NPDES Permit Conditions for Pulp Mill Discharge

## 2. Failure modes the skill is correcting

- Baseline identifies permit deficiencies without grounding each issue in the applicable regulatory standard, data methodology, or receiving-water protection requirement that the draft permit must satisfy
- Baseline fails to compare the draft permit against the existing permit to identify anti-backsliding concerns and changes requiring regulatory justification
- Baseline does not integrate available discharge monitoring data and engineering assessment findings to substantiate claims that permit limits are inadequate
- Baseline omits consultation correspondence and third-party submissions as sources of legally relevant objections that may strengthen the issues memorandum
- Baseline stops at spotting a defect instead of tying it to the governing rule, the supporting record, and the practical consequence for permit coverage or compliance

## 3. Legal frameworks / domain conventions that apply

- Clean Water Act permitting: distinguish technology-based effluent limits from water-quality-based effluent limits, and test whether the more stringent applicable limit is reflected in the draft permit
- NPDES implementing regulations: evaluate permit limits, monitoring, reporting, special conditions, and fact-sheet rationale under the applicable federal and state permitting rules
- Anti-backsliding: renewal permits should not become less stringent than prior permit conditions absent an applicable exception
- Water quality standards: state numeric and narrative criteria, designated uses, antidegradation policy, and any implementation procedures for translating criteria into permit conditions
- TMDLs: waste load allocations from applicable TMDLs should be incorporated into permit conditions where the receiving water is impaired or otherwise subject to allocation-based controls
- Mixing zone analysis: where allowed, assess whether the authorized zone is supported by the governing state method and is adequate for pollutants of concern
- Effluent guideline framework: identify the applicable technology-based standard for the facility type and operational subcategory
- Monitoring adequacy: sampling, analytical methods, frequency, and reporting must be sufficient to demonstrate compliance and support enforcement
- Administrative record review: fact sheets, technical memos, consultation letters, and public submissions can reveal analytical gaps or independently support objections

## 4. Analytical scaffolds

- Start by enumerating the draft conditions to be tested: effluent limits, monitoring requirements, reporting duties, special conditions, and any compliance schedules or variances
- Then compare each draft condition against the current permit, the applicable technology-based standard, the water-quality standard, and any relevant TMDL or mixing-zone support
- Use discharge monitoring data to assess whether the proposed limits track facility performance, whether they are overbroad, and whether the data show recurring noncompliance or margin concerns
- Use engineering assessment findings to test the technical basis for the draft permit; identify where the record supports a more protective limit or a different monitoring approach
- Review the fact sheet for missing assumptions, inconsistent calculations, unsupported selections, or omitted alternatives that weaken the legal basis for the draft condition
- Review consultation correspondence and public submissions for objections that map onto identifiable permit defects; use them to reinforce, not replace, the core regulatory argument
- For each issue, include: the permit condition, the controlling authority, the defect, the supporting record, the cross-reference to another source in the file set, the downstream consequence, and a comment position
- Treat a condition as vulnerable if the record does not explain why the selected limit, frequency, or method satisfies the governing rule in light of the facility’s discharge and receiving-water constraints

## 5. Vertical / structural / temporal relationships

- Existing permit as baseline: assess whether each corresponding draft condition is more or less stringent than the prior permit and whether any relaxation is justified under the governing anti-backsliding rule
- TMDL hierarchy: where a TMDL applies, verify that the draft permit aligns with the waste load allocation and any implementation method used by the permitting authority
- Record hierarchy: source documents may include technical data, agency analysis, correspondence, and submissions; if they conflict, note the conflict and identify which source best supports the comment position
- Temporal sequence matters: distinguish historic performance, current permit compliance, draft changes, and future compliance obligations so the memo does not conflate past events with proposed permit terms
- If multiple discharge points, pollutants, or permit condition groups are in scope, analyze each separately rather than blending them into a single generalized critique

## 6. Output structure conventions

- Organize the memo as a conventional issues memorandum with an executive summary, a categorized issue analysis section, a concise issues table, and a recommended public-comment approach
- Define an ordinal severity scale once at the outset and apply it consistently to every issue identified
- State each issue in a self-contained format: condition at issue, governing authority, defect, record support, cross-reference to related material, consequence, severity, and recommended comment
- Support every legal proposition by naming the controlling authority or regulatory provision on which the point depends
- When citing the record, identify the document type and the relevant data or analysis it contains; do not rely on unsupported generalizations
- Conclude with a recommended actions section that assigns an action, a responsible role, and a timing anchor tied to the comment deadline or permit process milestone
- Use the deliverable filename exactly as instructed: `permit-issue-identification-memo.docx`
