# Skill: Extract Indemnification Terms from Environmental Settlement Agreement — Indemnification Summary Memorandum

## 2. Failure modes the skill is correcting

- Reviewing only the main settlement document and missing terms buried in procedures, side letters, exhibits, or escrow documents
- Extracting the indemnity grant without mapping notice, defense-control, cooperation, timing, and payment mechanics that condition recovery
- Missing exclusions, carve-outs, or special treatment for identified matters that materially narrow the nominal indemnity
- Failing to reconcile the indemnity with any escrow, reserve, or other security package that backs it
- Treating all terms as equally important instead of separating core coverage, procedural conditions, limitations, and security support
- Stating conclusions about scope or priority without tying them to the controlling language of the integrated document set

## 3. Legal frameworks / domain conventions that apply

- Environmental settlement indemnification allocates cleanup, response, remediation, investigation, and related litigation costs by contract
- Claims procedures typically govern notice, supporting documentation, response timing, cooperation, defense or remediation control, and payment prerequisites
- Contractual exclusions and side-letter carve-outs can supersede the broad face of an indemnity grant for specified known matters
- Escrow and other security arrangements may limit or support practical recovery through funding, release conditions, investment terms, and trustee or agent duties
- Survival, termination, and limitation provisions determine how long indemnification rights and related procedures remain enforceable
- If the source set identifies governing-law or order-of-precedence language, it controls conflicts among the integrated documents
- Any legal proposition stated in the memo should be anchored to the governing text or a recognized contract-construction rule, not presented as a free-standing conclusion

## 4. Analytical scaffolds

- Treat the settlement package as one integrated transaction record; review the agreement, procedures, side letter, escrow terms, exhibits, and any incorporated schedules together
- Build the memo by issue category, not by document order: coverage grant, procedural prerequisites, exclusions/carve-outs, security package, priority/conflict rules, and practical risk
- First identify the universe of indemnifying and protected parties, then identify the universe of covered claim types, then map limitations and exceptions
- For each indemnity provision, extract: who indemnifies whom, what claims or losses are covered, what costs are included, whether defense or remediation control is allocated, and whether any cap, basket, deductible, or survival limit applies
- For each procedure provision, extract: notice trigger, notice contents, delivery method, timing, supplementation rights, cooperation duties, response deadlines, cure rights, and payment timing
- For each exclusion or carve-out, extract: the excluded matter, whether the exclusion is absolute or conditional, whether it is time-limited, and whether it shifts to another funding source
- For each escrow or security term, extract: deposit timing, funding source, permitted investments, release mechanics, dispute handling, draw conditions, replenishment, and termination
- Read all internal cross-references before summarizing any clause; where one document modifies another, reflect the modification rather than the base text alone
- If the set presents more than one covered claimant, claim type, time period, or security tranche, enumerate each explicitly and analyze each on its own terms rather than collapsing them into a generic summary

## 5. Vertical / structural / temporal relationships

- Document hierarchy: identify which instrument controls if the agreement conflicts with procedures, a side letter, or escrow terms
- Temporal sequencing: note when notice must be given, when a claim ripens, when a response is due, when funds may be released, and when obligations survive
- Claim layering: separate primary indemnity coverage from excluded or specially treated liabilities so the reader can see what is covered, what is reserved, and what is shifted elsewhere
- Security layering: distinguish the contractual indemnity promise from the practical recovery source that supports it
- If multiple related liabilities are carved out differently, preserve that structure instead of restating them as one blended exception

## 6. Output structure conventions

- Draft a concise but comprehensive indemnification summary memorandum with ordinary memo sections such as Executive Summary, Coverage and Indemnity Grant, Claims Process, Exclusions and Carve-Outs, Security and Escrow, Priority and Conflicts, and Practical Risk
- Use a table or similarly disciplined format for each distinct indemnity or carve-out, with columns for obligor, protected party, covered matters, limitations, procedure points, security support, and significance
- State any governing authority or controlling rule only where it is actually relevant to interpreting priority, survival, or procedure
- End with a short recommendations section identifying follow-up diligence, open points, and any clarifications that should be confirmed before reliance
- Ensure the filename matches the assigned deliverable exactly: indemnification-summary-memo.docx
